Norman Foster

The Architect of the Future

Here's a timeline of Norman Foster's life:

  • 1935 - Born in Reddish, Stockport, England.
  • 1953 - Completes National Service in the Royal Air Force, where his passion for aircraft and engineering begins.
  • 1961 - Graduates from the University of Manchester School of Architecture and City Planning.
  • 1962 - Wins a Henry Fellowship to Yale University, where he meets Richard Rogers and earns his Masterâ€™s degree.
  • 1963 - Co-founds the architectural practice "Team 4" with Richard Rogers and sisters Wendy and Georgie Cheesman.
  • 1967 - Establishes Foster Associates (now Foster + Partners) in London.
  • 1975 - Completes the Willis Building in Ipswich, a pioneering design that introduced open-plan offices and energy-saving glass facades.
  • 1985 - The HSBC Building in Hong Kong is completed; it is hailed as a masterpiece of "High-Tech" architecture for its modular, skeletal structure.
  • 1991 - Completes the Stansted Airport terminal, redefining the modern airport experience by placing all heavy machinery underground to allow for a light, airy roof.
  • 1997 - Appointed to the Order of Merit by Queen Elizabeth II.
  • 1999 - Receives the Pritzker Architecture Prize, the "Nobel Prize of architecture." He also completes the renovation of the Reichstag in Berlin, adding the famous glass dome.
  • 2004 - The 30 St Mary Axe (The Gherkin) opens in London, becoming an instant icon of the city's skyline.
  • 2004 - Completes the Millau Viaduct in France, a structural marvel that is higher than the Eiffel Tower.
  • 2017 - Unveils Apple Park in Cupertino, California, a massive ring-shaped campus powered entirely by renewable energy.
  • 2023 - Continues to advocate for "Sustainable Urbanism," focusing on how cities can survive the climate crisis through smart design and modular construction.

"As an architect, you design for the present, with an awareness of the past, for a future which is essentially unknown."

-- Norman Foster
